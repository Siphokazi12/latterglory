Programs

Infant Care

During this stage, infants are provided with specialized care tailored to their unique needs. Trained caregivers focus on feeding, diaper changing, and ensuring a safe and nurturing environment for the youngest members. Sensory stimulation and gentle interactions are key components of this stage to foster early development.

Baby/Toddler

As infants grow into toddlers, they become more mobile and curious. Caregivers in this stage focus on providing a stimulating environment that encourages exploration and development of fine and gross motor skills. Activities may include supervised play, simple crafts, and introduction to basic concepts like colors and shapes.

Toddler/Early Preschool

This stage emphasizes socialization and early learning experiences. Toddlers engage in structured activities designed to promote language development, cognitive skills, and social interaction. Storytime, music and movement, art projects, and outdoor play are integrated into the daily routine to encourage curiosity and creativity.

Preschool

Preschoolers are introduced to more structured learning experiences to prepare them for kindergarten. The curriculum focuses on foundational skills such as pre-reading, pre-writing, numeracy, and problem-solving. Play-based learning remains central, with activities designed to foster independence, confidence, and cooperation.

Grade R

In this final stage before entering formal schooling, children are prepared for the transition to kindergarten. The curriculum is more structured and academic, with a focus on readiness skills such as letter recognition, counting, writing, and social-emotional development. Pre-kindergarten programs often incorporate elements of literacy, math, science, and creative expression to ensure children are well-prepared for success in school.
